Design System Evolution

How a legacy enterprise fintech platform transitioned from scattered, inconsistent mockups into a unified, scalable design language — one practical step at a time. This was not a full redesign, but a strategic evolution that improved collaboration, quality, and delivery speed across Product and Engineering.

Role
UX Designer & System Owner
Timeline
Ongoing
Platform
Figma / Web App
Deliverables
Audit, System, Components, Mockups

The Background

Over several years, the product had grown feature by feature, team by team. Each initiative brought new mockups—built with different tools, spacing rules, form layouts, and interaction patterns. Nothing was deliberately inconsistent; the organization simply didn't have a shared design foundation.

As the product matured and the pace increased, these inconsistencies began creating friction: unclear handoffs, mismatched expectations, and mockups that didn’t always reflect the real product.

Placeholder Image / Design Audit

The Challenge

Different designers and product managers created mockups in different ways. Engineering had no single source of truth for UI patterns. Meetings often began with clarifying “what’s the real version?” instead of discussing solutions.

The team needed a consistent, shared design language—something lightweight, practical, and easy for everyone to adopt without slowing delivery.

Placeholder Image / Fragmented Mockups

My Approach

01 — Auditing the Existing UI

I mapped out the most commonly used screens—billing, customer management, configuration—identifying where inconsistencies created confusion or slowed development.

Placeholder Image / Audit Findings

02 — Creating a Shared Visual Language

I introduced Figma as our primary design environment and built a practical, lightweight component library: buttons, form fields, table patterns, spacing rules, and layout structures—just enough to solve our most immediate needs.

Placeholder Image / Component Library

03 — Elevating Mockup Fidelity

With reusable components in place, mockups became clearer, higher fidelity, and far more aligned with the actual product. Product managers could contribute content directly, and Engineering finally had a consistent reference.

Placeholder Image / Before & After Mockups

04 — Scaling Across New Features

As the design system stabilized, new features became faster to design and easier to review. Instead of reinventing UI patterns for each project, I could focus on refining flows and solving customer problems.

Placeholder Image / Feature Screens

The Impact

  • Clear, consistent mockups across all new work
  • Faster collaboration between Product and Engineering
  • A unified design source of truth in Figma
  • Reusable components that reduced design and development time
  • A scalable system future designers can continue to build upon
Placeholder Image / System Impact

This wasn’t a big-bang redesign—it was a steady, deliberate evolution. By introducing structure where it mattered, we improved quality and collaboration without slowing delivery.
