Salesforce Connector

When this project began, the company found itself at a pivotal moment. Their customers relied heavily on Salesforce, yet every integration required custom API development which was costly, brittle, and inaccessible to the people who needed it most. Instead of empowering sales teams, the process slowed them down. I saw an opportunity to reshape that experience from the ground up.

Role
Product & UX Lead
Timeline
6 Months
Platform
Salesforce Lightning
Deliverables
Research, UX, Requirements, QA, PM

The Problem

Every customer implementation was starting from zero. If a sales team wanted Salesforce and the enterprise billing solution provider to work together, they needed a developer, either in-house or outsourced, to stitch APIs together. These integrations often broke, causing inconsistent billing data and endless operational headaches.

The experience wasn’t just inefficient, it was undermining trust. Sales leaders wanted a seamless subscription workflow inside Salesforce. Admins needed predictable syncing behaviors. And the enterprise billing solution provider needed a way to deliver this without building everything customer by customer. A native Salesforce app wasn’t just a feature; it was a strategic necessity.

My Approach

I started by stepping into the shoes of everyone involved, sales teams, admins, support staff, and implementation consultants. Through interviews and shadowing sessions, a pattern quickly emerged, people weren’t asking for more features, they were asking for clarity, reliability, and control.

With these insights, I led the creation of a streamlined Salesforce native configuration experience. My goal was to give admins a sense of empowerment, clear mapping rules, straightforward setup flows, and predictable syncing behaviors that didn't require a developer lurking in the background.

I collaborated closely with an external development team, translating business requirements into technical specifications and managing sprints in an agile cadence. Every week, I performed QA on deliverables, ensuring the sync logic, UI components, and API interactions held up against real customer scenarios.

The Solution

The result was the enterprise billing solution provider's first-ever Salesforce native integration app and an experience that finally met customers where they worked.

  • A clean, Salesforce native configuration UI that removed the need for custom code
  • Clear sync rules and validation logic that protected data integrity
  • Error handling states that made troubleshooting accessible for non technical teams
  • Automated workflows for account, product, and subscription syncing
  • A documented object mapping framework used by both customers and internal teams

The Outcome

The final product didn’t just reduce integration time, it transformed the onboarding experience entirely. Sales teams could now manage subscriptions directly within Salesforce. Admins finally had visibility and control. And for the enterprise billing solution provider, what was once a custom, inconsistent integration became a scalable product offering.

Most importantly, the connector shifted customer perception. Instead of fighting the limitations of APIs, teams felt supported, empowered, and confident that their data would stay in sync.

The app is now live on the Salesforce AppExchange, providing customers with a reliable, intuitive integration that no longer requires a developer to maintain.
